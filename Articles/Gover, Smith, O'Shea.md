- *Auditory icons* - everyday sounds to convey info from computer systems
	- Useful in complex systems where users cannot expect to notice all visual feedback
- Aim for the study: 
	1. Application in complex environments
	2. Auditory cues to assist collaboration
	3. Convey information about many events happening at the same time
- Used a simulation of a softdrink bottle plant to do this
- Concluded that auditory feedback provided participants the cues needed to diagnose problems/monitor the plant and machines

## Useful?
- Yes, as this is proof that a simulation is viable to the actual, real-life thing.  Especially for the simulation of the Paris Waterflow and using haptics/game engine tech like Unity/ThreeJS to simulate it.  This would be evidence/backing that you can use audio cues to aid in collaboration/problem diagnosing.
